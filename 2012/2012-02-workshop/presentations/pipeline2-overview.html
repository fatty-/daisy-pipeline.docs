DAISY Pipeline 2 Overview

Objectives and Roadmap

February 16, 2011 - Romain Deltour

What is it ?

an open-source, cross-platform framework for the automated production and maintenance of accessible content, efficiently and economically

At the origins, a community

DAISY: an international community

  • including a wide range of user groups
  • facing an increasing demand for content
  • in a variety of formats
  • for a variety of devices

Looking for great tooling

  • efficiency

    ↪ automated production of content

  • versatility

    ↪ production, publication and maintenance of XML-based document formats

    ↪ interoperability

    ↪ extensibility

  • quality

    ↪ good authoring and accessibility practices

    ↪ validation throughout the production chain

  • low cost

    ↪ reusability, no duplication of effort

    ↪ use of standards and open source code

A tale of two Pipelines

DAISY Pipeline project

  • started in 2006
  • successful adoption
  • some star transformers: Narrator, DTBook Fix, validators
  • ... but some technical limitations, and increasing maintenance cost

DAISY Pipeline 2 project

  • a complete redesign
  • kicked-off in June 2010

  • chartered for 3 years

    ↪ Phase 1: June 2010 to September 2011

    ↪ Phase 2: October 2011 to September 2013

Development model

  • Open source

    ↪ LGPL: weak copyleft license, commercial friendly

  • Collaborative

    ↪ developed and funded by DAISY member organizations (thanks!)

  • Core team of 5 developers

    ↪ 3 from DAISY, 1 from NLB, 1 from SBS

  • + Braille Working Group

    ↪ approximately 10 active participants

Architecture

  • a framework and runtime engine
  • a set of processing modules ("scripts")

Feature highlights: Framework

  • based on standard XML processing technologies (XProc, XSLT)

    ↪ open and platform-neutral

    ↪ reduces development and maintenance cost

  • designed with a modular architecture

    ↪ based on a service-oriented, dynamic module system

    ↪ provides good scalability and extensibility

  • built-in interoperability

    ↪ web service API as a primary entry point

    ↪ support the invocation of third-party executables and web services

Feature highlights: Scripts

  • support the new DAISY/NISO Z39.98-2012 "Authoring and Interchange" standard (aka "Zed AI")
  • focus on accessible EPUB 3 production
  • promote single-source publishing
